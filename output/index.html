Jegi-dong station tops seoul's senior visitor rankings near herbal medicine material market

12 May 2026 · chosun.com

Jegi-dong station in Seoul ranks first among the city's stations for the proportion of senior passengers, a distinction closely tied to its proximity to major retail and social destinations for older Koreans. The station's Exit 2 provides the most direct access to Seoul Yangnyeongsi [GLOSSARY GAP], the country's largest herbal medicine material market, and to Gyeongdong Market. The surrounding area is densely concentrated with facilities catering to older demographics, including budget restaurants, barbershops, and dance halls. For senior visitors, the precinct functions not merely as a shopping destination but as a social hub where they meet friends and spend leisure time.

Korean association of oriental medicine calls for korean medicine to become a national strategic industry

6 May 2026 · yonhapnewstv.co.kr

The Korean Association of Oriental Medicine issued a statement on 6 July calling on the government to designate Korean traditional medicine as a national strategic industry, citing surging demand from foreign visitors. Statistics from the Ministry of Health and Welfare show that foreign patients visiting Korean medicine clinics reached 37,087 in 2025, up 9.4% from 34,535 in 2024, while total foreign patients in South Korea exceeded two million for the first time, reaching 2,011,822. The association attributed part of this growth to the global popularity of the 2025 drama Kdeheon [GLOSSARY GAP], in which the lead character visits a Korean medicine clinic to receive herbal medicine treatment from a Korean medicine doctor — a storyline widely credited with raising the international profile of Korean traditional medicine. The Seoul Korean Medicine Promotion Centre, which served as the real-world model for the clinic featured in the drama, recorded 1,720 foreign visitors in June 2025, a 50.9% increase on the previous month. Beyond clinical treatment, Korean traditional medicine is expanding into medical tourism and cultural experience, with activities such as foot baths, herbal teas, and visits to herbal medicine material markets drawing foreign visitors. The Korea International Trade Association projects the global traditional medicine market will reach ₩1,125 trillion by 2027. The Korean Association of Oriental Medicine argued that regulatory barriers continue to constrain the sector despite explosive growth in foreign demand, and called on the government to designate Korean traditional medicine as a national medical tourism brand and develop an integrated industry model — encompassing licensing, export support, and overseas market entry — linked with K-beauty, K-pop, wellness programmes, and regional tourism.

Jung Kwan Jang owner secures trademark rights across 71 countries

26 May 2026 · mt.co.kr

Korea Ginseng Corporation (KGC), the parent company of Jung Kwan Jang, has emerged as the leading intellectual property (IP) manager in Korea's health functional food industry, registering <strong>1,866 trademark applications over the ten-year period from 2013 to 2023</strong> — the highest of any food sector company, according to a trademark application trend report published by the Korean Intellectual Property Office (Korea's national IP administration body). Total food-sector trademark filings over the same period reached 241,467. KGC's strategy of building a long-term trademark portfolio to strengthen brand competitiveness in the health functional food market is credited for this result. KGC currently holds 3,930 domestic and 4,537 overseas trademark registrations, bringing its total portfolio to <strong>8,467 registrations across 71 countries</strong>. Korea, China, Taiwan, Japan, and the United States together account for 68% of all registered marks. KGC's IP strategy centres on securing rights ahead of market entry rather than reacting to infringement after the fact. The protected portfolio extends well beyond company and brand names to cover the corporate identity, the Jung Kwan Jang brand identity, emblem logos, product design elements, and key product names including CheonNok. A KGC spokesperson stated that competitive advantage in global markets is now determined by brand and IP strength rather than manufacturing capability alone, and urged K-food companies more broadly to pre-emptively secure trademark and design rights before entering overseas markets.

Why it matters KGC's aggressive global trademark strategy, including coverage of CheonNok, signals the competitive IP environment New Zealand deer velvet exporters and brand partners must navigate.

Korean pharmacy tablet uses Russian deer antler collagen, bypassing health functional food status

25 May 2026 · 

Hanpure [PROPER NOUN — verify romanisation] has listed a general food product — Hanpure Deer Antler Low-Molecular Collagen — available through pharmacies in South Korea. The product is a pink, oblong, film-coated tablet supplied in packs of 30, with a recommended dosage of one tablet per day taken with water. The <strong>key functional ingredient is low-molecular deer antler collagen</strong>, derived from deer antler sourced from Russia and hydrolysed using protease of Danish origin. Additional ingredients include vitamin C, D-sorbitol, hyaluronic acid in a trehalose blend, hydrolysed elastin sourced from France, glutathione yeast extract from Estonia, white tomato concentrated extract powder from the USA, a blend of 18 amino acids, and red yeast rice pigment. Because the product is classified as a general food rather than a health functional food, it carries no approved functional claims under Korean regulatory standards. Storage instructions require avoidance of temperatures above 30°C, relative humidity above 75%, and direct sunlight. Consumers with allergies or atopic constitutions are advised to verify ingredients before use, and the label cautions that excessive intake may cause diarrhoea owing to a <strong>40% D-sorbitol content</strong>.

Why it matters Russian-origin deer antler is displacing New Zealand supply in Korean collagen tablet formats sold through mainstream pharmacy channels.

hy researcher wins ministerial award for fermented deer velvet and probiotics ingredient

13 May 2026 · webeconomy.co.kr

hy has announced that senior researcher Hong Dong-gi received the Minister of Science and ICT Award at the 2026 Science and Technology Promotion Commendation Ceremony, held on 13 May 2026. The award recognises his development of 'fermented deer velvet probiotics powder', a health functional food ingredient produced by fermenting deer velvet with a proprietary probiotics strain (L. curvatus HY7602). The ingredient holds the distinction of being <strong>the only individually approved raw material in Korea</strong> for deer velvet-based muscle strength maintenance. A human clinical trial confirmed its efficacy, with the intervention group recording a <strong>20.4% improvement in average grip strength</strong> compared to the control group.

Why it matters Individually approved status for a fermented deer velvet and probiotics ingredient strengthens functional-food positioning and may expand demand for New Zealand deer velvet in Korean B2B supply chains.
